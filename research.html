Player Programmed Partner Games

P3G is a project aimed at addressing the lack of robotics and coding education for low-resource learners. I worked with fellow CMU students to brainstorm and prototype a wide variety of games in pursuit of this goal, and eventually narrrowed the ideas down to a final proposal for a game called Sunlight; however, due to the pandemic, we had to transition to a new mode of research, cultural probes. We are using these probes to learn more about the way children think while engaging them with co-robotics. Below is an example of a probe card I created, in addition to a sketch of possible orders of categories of probes to send to the children.




VR Cognition Study


This study examines the effects of immersive virtual experiences and exercise on cognition and neuroplasticity in adults. To do so, we created a VR game similar to the popular game Beat Saber, but with conditions for different physical and cognitive tasks. I also worked on developing an in-game questionnaire in Unity for subjects to take after participating in the study.
